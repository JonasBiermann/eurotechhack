# script

Today in Hong Kong, more than 700,000 residents over the age of 60 live in subsidised housing.
Many of them carry the same quiet worries every day. The fear of being priced out of the city they grew up in. The struggle to reach a doctor where the wait for elderly care can stretch from twelve to twenty months. And the challenge of navigating a city that keeps changing around them.
The financial reality is tight. Average retirement savings in Hong Kong sit at around HK$350,000 set against monthly living costs of fifteen to twenty thousand, that cushion comes under real pressure in one of the most expensive cities in the world.
And the pressure is only growing. Hong Kong's largest generation is now in their fifties and sixties, while the generation behind them is far smaller.
This is why nearly 90,000 Hong Kong seniors already live across the border in Guangdong. It is close to home, noticeably cheaper, and still well suited to daily senior life.
The government has noticed. It set up programmes like the Residential Care Services Scheme in Guangdong to make that move easier. But these schemes are still young; today, only around a thousand seniors use them. Between the tens of thousands who move on their own and the handful the system formally supports, there is an enormous gap.
That gap is where our product lives.
安居, meaning "settle in peace, dwell securely", is an end-to-end platform for both the seniors making this decision and the government officials who process the paperwork for moving into mainland China. 
It is a SaaS platform that the HK government and the NGOs already running the Guangdong care schemes pay for, while it stays free for seniors and their families. We go to market by piloting with those scheme operators first, since they already have seniors who want to move, and then expand to the other cross-border elderly-care programs from there. 
What makes it special is how personal it is. 安居 recommends where to move based on each person's finances, medical needs, mobility, and what they value most in daily life.
It is built directly on Hong Kong's Smart City data, drawing on healthcare access, transport links, and building information to ground every recommendation and help seniors decide quickly and with confidence.
And above all, it keeps communities together. By learning what seniors in each part of the city share, 安居 moves people from similar backgrounds and neighbourhoods to the same places, so the social network that took a lifetime to build survives the move.
Our goal is simple. To let Hong Kong's seniors live the retirement they have earned, free from the constant worry of being priced out, of struggling to reach a doctor, of disappearing into an overcrowded city. Where care that can take well over a year at home is within reach far sooner.
And by supporting those who choose to move, we ease the strain on Hong Kong's housing, shortening the wait for the families still in the queue.
安居. A place to settle. A community to belong to.
